Beyond Trees

Forest ecology has long centered on trees. Beyond Trees explores the diversity of growth forms that climb, sprawl, twine, and hang, and the vital roles they play in forests as ecological and socioeconomic systems.

Our mission

Seeing beyond the trees

Forests are not made of trees alone.

Palms, bamboos, bananas, lianas, climbers, and epiphytes shape tropical ecosystems alongside trees, yet they remain underrepresented in ecological research and conservation discourse.

These growth forms reveal alternative solutions to the woody tree strategy for some of the most fundamental challenges of life on land: reaching the canopy, persisting through time, competing for light, and surviving disturbance. Their evolutionary pathways differ dramatically from those of woody trees, but their ecological importance is no less profound.

Beyond Trees is a research community dedicated to understanding, celebrating, and fostering research on the diverse growth forms that make forests possible. By broadening our perspective beyond trees, we hope to build a more inclusive understanding of forest ecology, biodiversity, and sustainability.
